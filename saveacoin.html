PROJECT
OVERVIEW

About

Save-a-Coin is a group project completed for the User Centered Design course at the University of Central Florida. It is a money-saving and tracking app designed to help users take control of their finances with ease and confidence.

Key Features

  • Create a customized profile tailored to your financial goals, location, preferred currency, number format, and more.
  • Link your bank account for easier spending and savings tracking. This feature automatically fetches transaction details, eliminating the need for manual input.
  • Categorize and monitor your expenses in a way that works for you. The simple interface allows for seamless navigation and a clear overview of your financial activities.
  • Create savings categories, set achievable goals, and track your progress in real-time by linking your savings account.
  • Discover coupons and deals from your favorite stores for online and in-person shopping. Filter your search with categories.

Timeline

The process for this project began in September 2024. It included multiple steps, each accompanied by a presentation summarizing conducted research, documentation, and completed design. Presentation of the final product took place in December 2024.

PROCESS

Discovery Phase

To lay the foundation for the Save-a-Coin app, we conducted comprehensive research into the most popular budget tracking and saving apps on the market. We analyzed 12 leading apps, including Goodbudget, Ibotta, Monarch, and others. Our goal was to identify the strengths and weaknesses of each app, focusing on usability, feature set, availability, and reach.

To synthesize our findings, we created a Function Matrix to map out essential features and identify areas where existing apps fell short. This process guided our brainstorming sessions and informed our approach to designing a comprehensive and user-friendly money-saving experience.

Research

The next step in the project was to identify our target audience and stakeholders. After thorough analysis, we determined that the primary users of the Save-a-Coin app are adults, college students, families, and online shoppers, focusing on individuals seeking financial control, expense tracking, and efficient saving strategies. The key stakeholders include investors, business partners, competitor apps, the stock market, developers, and app employees.

To better understand our users, we created three detailed personas to visualize their needs and preferences.

Design Process

Our objective in the design phase was to create a user-friendly and visually appealing interface that meet accessibility guidelines and would make using the application seamless and intuitive. Figma was our main design and collaboration tool at this stage of the project.

Throughout the design process, we engaged with potential users to gather feedback on our prototypes. This feedback was crucial in refining the design before finalizing the screens.

Development Process

The goal of the development phase was to turn our designs into a fully functional app that is robust, responsive, and scalable. Working in React Native ensured a smooth and native-like experience across both iOS and Android platforms.

Firebase was selected as our backend database solution for its real-time data synchronization and ease of integration with React Native.

DESIGN
SYSTEM

Logo

Logo was something our team experimented with a lot. From trying out different colors and typefaces to completely reestablishing the main elements. The initial logo features a lot of different animal outlines in black color with the name "Petter" underneath. However, it seemed a bit outdated and not as catchy.

We wanted to create a simple logo but still incorporate a color palette that would feature pets as the primary goal of our brand. After brainstorming and making several new sketches to best represent what our app is about, we decided to use a minimalist dog profile outline resembling a letter "P," which then became the new face of PETTER.

Other Design Elements

Our objective for the additional design elements was to ensure that every aspect of PETTER's interface was not only functional but also visually cohesive and aligned with the overall aesthetic and usability goals.

Throughout the design process, we incorporated feedback from user testing to ensure these elements not only looked good but also enhanced the overall functionality of the app. Our design choices were consistently tested for accessibility to make sure they meet the latest standards, ensuring that PETTER is inclusive for all users.

OUTCOMES

Challenges

Throughout the development of PETTER, we encountered several challenges that required iteration, testing, and problem-solving to ensure the best possible user experience. From UI complexity to backend issues, these obstacles pushed us to refine our design and improve functionality.

During early design iterations, during user testing, some reported several screens being cluttered, containing too much information, making users feel overwhelmed and unsure where to focus. Moreover, some users reported difficulty locating important features, such as the "Vets" section, due to its placement in the app. This lack of intuitive navigation created friction in the user journey.

During development, we faced issues with Firebase integration, leading to unexpected bugs that temporarily prevented some core functionalities from working properly. This particularly affected data synchronizatio and scheduling features.

By identifying these challenges early and iterating based on user feedback, we were able to enhance PETTER’s usability and performance. The result was a more refined, accessible, and seamless experience that effectively meets the needs of pet owners.
